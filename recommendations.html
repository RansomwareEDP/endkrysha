The Reading Room

Recom­mendations

A working shelf for anyone trying to understand the ransomware protection economy: the money, the metal, and the roof over both. Books, podcasts, primary-source reports, and the feeds we check daily. Suggestions and additions are welcome, the list is meant to grow.

✦Listen First · Highly Recommended
Highly Recommended · New Season Ongoing
Cyber Hack
BBC World Service · Podcast · The team behind The Lazarus Heist

If this site is for you, so is this show. The BBC's investigative cybercrime strand works the same ground End Krysha maps: the crews, the cash-out, and the roof over both, told through leaked chats, court records, and firsthand reporting. Start with the two seasons below.

Season 4 · Ongoing
The Conti Files

Built from the leaked Conti chats: a ransomware gang run like a company, with payroll, HR, and hospitals on the victim list. New episodes weekly.

Season 3
Evil Corp

The Yakubets family business and its state ties: the clearest public telling of the krysha in action. Pairs directly with our seven-part Bloodline series.

01Books · The Long Read

Narrative nonfiction that builds the mental model: how Russian and CIS crews operate, how the cash-out works, and why takedowns so often fail to stick.

/ 01
Sandworm
Andy Greenberg · 2019
The definitive account of Russia's GRU-linked hacking unit and the NotPetya attack, the most destructive cyberattack in history. The clearest illustration of state-directed offensive operations and the impunity that follows them.
The Krysha
View →
/ 02
The Ransomware Hunting Team
Renee Dudley & Daniel Golden · 2022
A band of volunteer researchers who break ransomware and help victims recover without paying. Grounds the abstract ecosystem in the people fighting it and the technical realities of the malware itself.
Threat Actors
View →
/ 03
Tracers in the Dark
Andy Greenberg · 2022
How investigators learned to follow cryptocurrency that was assumed to be untraceable, straight to the people behind it. Essential for understanding the Money layer: where the cash-out breaks and how it gets exposed.
The Money
View →
/ 04
Spam Nation
Brian Krebs · 2014
Older but foundational: the inside story of Russian organized cybercrime and the pharma-spam empires that built the playbook. The cast of characters and the protection dynamics map directly onto today's ransomware economy.
The Krysha
View →
/ 05
Fancy Bear Goes Phishing
Scott J. Shapiro · 2023
A historian-of-ideas approach to five landmark hacks, including the GRU breach of the DNC. Strong on why systems stay insecure and how attacker incentives, not just code, shape outcomes.
Foundations
View →
/ 06
This Is How They Tell Me the World Ends
Nicole Perlroth · 2021
The global market for software exploits, the raw material that fuels intrusions. Context for the supply side of the ecosystem and how vulnerabilities become weapons.
Foundations
View →
/ 07
Countdown to Zero Day
Kim Zetter · 2014
The Stuxnet story, told with rigor. A useful baseline for how nation-state cyber operations are planned, deployed, and attributed, which informs how to read the protection layer.
Foundations
View →
02Reports & Primary Sources

The tracking numbers and policy frameworks behind the analysis. Treat these as the data layer: cite them, do not invent figures.

/ 01
Chainalysis Crypto Crime Report
Chainalysis · Annual
The most-cited on-chain measurement of ransomware payment volumes, year over year. The reference point for sizing the Money layer and tracking whether pressure is working.
The Money
View →
/ 02
Ransomware Task Force: Combating Ransomware
Institute for Security and Technology · 2021, with annual progress reports
The cornerstone policy blueprint: 48 recommendations across Deter, Detect, Prepare, Respond. The progress reports are a useful scorecard on what governments have and have not done.
Policy
View →
/ 03
CISA #StopRansomware Advisories
CISA / FBI / partners · Ongoing
Joint advisories with indicators of compromise, TTPs, and named-group profiles. Primary-source technical detail straight from US government incident response.
Infrastructure
View →
/ 04
TRM Labs Reports & Whitepapers
TRM Labs · Ongoing
Blockchain intelligence reporting on illicit crypto flows: ransomware financing, sanctioned exchanges, and cross-chain laundering. A second on-chain vantage point to check against Chainalysis figures.
The Money

03Ongoing Reading · Feeds & Reporting

Where the news actually breaks. Daily and weekly sources worth a standing tab.

/ 01
KrebsOnSecurity
Brian Krebs · Blog
Deep, named investigations into cybercrime operators and infrastructure, frequently the first to attribute. Strong on the human network behind the brands.
